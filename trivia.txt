Cappuccino - Equal parts espresso, steamed milk and foamed milk (about 60 ml each) - Sprinkle chocolate on top of the coffee

Mocha - 60 ml of espresso, 60 ml of chocolate and 30 ml of steamed milk

Latte - generally just a 60 ml shot of espresso with 300 ml steamed milk — and only 2ml foamed milk

Cafe Au Lait - If you prefer brewed coffee over espresso, cafe au lait is for you. It’s just a pampered cup of coffee — calling for warm, steamed milk in place of cold milk.

Americano - It’s a shot of espresso (30 ml) with twice the amount of water (60 ml).

Cafe Noisette - The noisette is one step between the machiatto and the latte. It’s a big shot of espresso with half of that amount in hot milk.

Macchiato - macchiato you get a shot of espresso (or sometimes two) with just a dot of foamed milk.

Doppio - Doppio means double in Italian, and that’s exactly what you get with espresso. Rather than the expected 30 ml shot of espresso, a doppio gets you a 60 ml shot.

Espresso - A small shot of caffeine — but more than enough to get the job done — usually about 30 ml.

Ristretto -  ristretto is the coffee choice for those who like to get straight to the point. It’s a very concentrated espresso shot — only 22 ml. / an espresso shot that is extracted with the same amount of coffee but half the amount of water. The end result is a more concentrated and darker espresso extraction. 

Mocha - A mocha is a mix between a cappuccino and a hot chocolate. It is made by putting mixing chocolate powder with an espresso shot and then adding steamed milk and micro-foam into the beverage. 

Affogato - An affogato is a simple dessert coffee that is treat during summer and after dinner. It is made by placing one big scoope of vanilla ice cream within a single or double shot of espresso:
